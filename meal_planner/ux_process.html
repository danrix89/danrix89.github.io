MyMealPlanner Web App

The MyMealPlanner web app was designed to help those who plan out monthly dinner calendars for their families. The following will guide you through the process I used to research, design, build, and test my application. Click on the links in each section to see the supporting documentation.
Enjoy.

Where to start?

I began by first by getting to know who my target audience was, what interfaces they were using, and how they interacted with those interfaces.
Before I could even think of designing, I needed data.

Preliminary Analysis

Know thy user

Second, I gathered more precise data about my target audience like, how tech savvy they are, their personalities, and other defining details. After I compiled the data I pieced together the commonalities into general a persona and scenario. Then I used those to build a heuristic analysis of my users' needs and wants of the desired interface. All throughout this process I continued to refine my data with more user interaction.

Data & Criteria

Design iterations

Once I knew the users' needs I was able to start sketching up "breadth-first" ideas and testing them against my criteria. As a design met criteria, I'd iterate deeper and deeper until a design was stable. I then built high fidelity prototypes with a detailed specification.

Prototype Spec

Build it and they will come

At this point, I was ready to build it. I followed my specification, but made sure to refine as I went along by sampling my work with users and innocent bystanders.

Implementation Spec

The home stretch

The time had come to put my app to the test. I handed it over to a qualified peer who created a usability plan and let the users use it. The results came back with great news. My design was generally accepted. There were only a few small tweaks and polish points I had to make, but other than that, my hard work paid off.

Final Spec

It's alive!

Since that point, I've continued to refine and polish the app based on user feedback. Little by little the app has become more and more what the users both want and need.

Feel free to check it out and have fun!
